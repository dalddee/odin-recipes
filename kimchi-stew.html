Kimchi Stew

Description

A traditional Korean stew that entails one of the most pivotal ingredients in Korean cuisine: Kimchi. A warm, delicious stew with a little kick is a dish that reminds me of home.

Ingredients

  • kimchi (the more fermented, the better)
  • minced garlic
  • onions
  • red pepper flakes
  • red pepper paste (not necessary)
  • meat of your choice (pork belly is most commonly used)
  • peppers
  • green onions
  • water
  • tofu
  • salt/soy sauce if needed

Steps

  1. Prepare the ingredients. Chop the kimchi, onions, peppers, tofu, and green onions into preferred sizes.
  2. Cook the kimchi and meat (if using) on medium-high heat with a bit of cooking oil. Mixing in a bit of sesame oil is suggested if available.

    3. In this process, you can add the red pepper flakes, garlic, red pepper paste, and soy sauce, or you can add them later after adding water
  3. After the kimchi and meat have relatively cooked where the kimchi appears to have 'softened' a bit, add the water and bring it to a boil.
  4. Once the stew has begun to boil, add more red pepper flakes/garlic/salt/soy sauce as necessary. Adding more red pepper paste is not recommended in this step.
  5. You can cook the stew for as long as you want. The longer you cook it, the deeper the flavors. Add the tofu and green onions a few minutes (10-20) before serving (with a bowl of white rice! XD)
